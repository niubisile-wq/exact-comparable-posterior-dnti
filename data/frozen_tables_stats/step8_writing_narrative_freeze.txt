Frozen contribution statements
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Contribution 1: Exact-comparable amortized posterior inference.
  Claim:
    A neural posterior approximation can track exact-enumeration posterior
    references while reducing online inference cost.
  Evidence:
    Fig. 2, Fig. 6, Table A, Table E.
  Frozen numbers:
    33-bus: NRE top-1 68.58%, exact 72.10%, speedup 1176x.
    69-bus: NRE top-1 36.05%, exact 40.20%, speedup 3302x.
    119-bus: NRE top-1 41.90%, exact 52.80%, speedup 3762x.
    Posterior quality on 33-bus: calibrated NRE top-1 68.37%, KL(reference||NRE)
    0.045329, ECE 0.0181, 90% credible-set coverage 97.62%.
  Boundary:
    Do not claim NRE always matches exact accuracy. The 119-bus approximation
    gap is material and must be disclosed.

Contribution 2: Robust posterior inference under missing measurements.
  Claim:
    The IP-C training/evaluation protocol improves robustness under missing
    measurements and outage-induced measurement loss.
  Evidence:
    Fig. 3, Table C, ST1, S1.
  Frozen numbers:
    33-bus @10% missing: robust 65.18%, naive 40.10%, delta +25.08pp.
    33-bus @30% missing: robust 61.01%, naive 10.10%, delta +50.91pp.
    69-bus @10% missing: robust 33.02%, naive 20.80%, delta +12.22pp.
    69-bus @30% missing: robust 30.56%, naive 7.30%, delta +23.26pp.
    Outage-induced missing, 33-bus: robust 62.97%, naive 32.35%, delta +30.62pp.
  Boundary:
    This is missing-measurement and outage-induced missing handling, not full
    outage/fault diagnosis or topology-change localization.

Contribution 3: Posterior-aware sensor placement.
  Claim:
    Posterior uncertainty can be used for sensor-placement decisions; BOED is
    competitive on 33-bus, stronger on the current 69-bus case, and adaptive
    BOED is strong under fair adaptive 119-bus comparisons.
  Evidence:
    Fig. 4, Table D.
  Frozen numbers:
    33-bus K=7: BOED 75.20%, MVG 75.40%, Random 41.80%.
    69-bus K=7: BOED 71.50%, MVG 66.50%, Random 24.00%.
    119-bus K=7 adaptive: AdaptiveBOED 70.00%, AdaptiveFisher 58.30%,
    AdaptiveMVG 56.70%.
  Boundary:
    Do not state or imply universal BOED dominance. State the observed
    case-specific pattern and the static/adaptive comparison boundary.

Contribution 4: Identifiability, statistics, baselines, and scalability gates.
  Claim:
    The evidence chain is statistically checked, compared with a strong GNN
    point-estimate baseline, and extended to a synthetic 300-bus mid-scale
    stress case.
  Evidence:
    Fig. 1, Fig. 2, Fig. 5, Table B, Table F, ST1.
  Frozen numbers:
    Primary six planned Wilcoxon tests use n=8 33/69-bus files.
    BH-FDR: 6/6 PASS. Holm: 6/6 PASS. Bonferroni: 6/6 PASS.
    GraphSAGE: 33-bus 68.13% vs NRE 68.58%; 69-bus 29.27% vs NRE 36.05%.
    Synthetic 300-bus: IP1 NRE 49.38%, exact 53.60%, NRE inference 0.004806 ms,
    exact 0.184350 ms, speedup 40.2x.
    Synthetic 300-bus IP-C 30% missing: NRE 26.87%, exact 42.60%.
  Boundary:
    300-bus is synthetic fixed-deployment mid-scale scalability evidence, not
    real utility full-scale validation. The 300-bus IP-C result is a stress
    boundary, not a strong robustness claim.
