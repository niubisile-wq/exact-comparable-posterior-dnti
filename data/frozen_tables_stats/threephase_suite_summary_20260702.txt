Three-phase benchmark suite summary (2026-07-02)

Benchmarks
1. 10-bus directional extension
- source: step6_3ph_result.txt
- n_topologies = 9
- deployment = K=6 fixed observed buses
- mean exact top-1 = 0.3840
- mean NRE top-1 = 0.3200
- mean gap = 0.0640
- mean speedup = 413998x
- role: initial directional extension showing that the NRE posterior framework carries over to an unbalanced three-phase toy feeder.

2. 13-bus fixed-deployment bridge benchmark
- source: threephase_13bus_fixed_result.txt
- n_topologies = 17
- deployment = K=8 fixed observed buses
- mean exact top-1 = 0.1160
- mean NRE top-1 = 0.1173
- mean gap = -0.0013
- mean KL(ref||NRE) = 0.0055
- mean speedup = 45.9x
- role: intermediate bridge benchmark showing near-exact posterior tracking on a highly ambiguous three-phase topology library.

3. 37-bus fixed-deployment stronger benchmark
- source: threephase_37bus_fixed_result.txt
- n_topologies = 56
- deployment = K=24 fixed observed buses
- mean exact top-1 = 0.5780
- mean NRE top-1 = 0.5087
- mean gap = 0.0693
- mean KL(ref||NRE) = 0.1681
- mean speedup = 85.6x
- role: stronger reviewer-facing three-phase benchmark beyond toy scale.

Interpretation
- The 10-bus and 37-bus benchmarks both show nontrivial exact-vs-NRE gaps of roughly 6-7 percentage points while preserving clear online speedup.
- The 13-bus benchmark is unusually ambiguous in top-1 terms, but the learned posterior tracks the exact posterior essentially exactly, indicating that the low absolute top-1 is driven by posterior ambiguity rather than obvious approximation failure.
- Taken together, the package now contains a small three-phase suite rather than a single toy extension.

Boundary
- All three benchmarks remain synthetic and are not a substitute for a utility-grade real-feeder three-phase benchmark.
- The suite materially reduces the prior reviewer attack that the paper had only a toy three-phase check.
